Stage 1 — AI Review (Gatekeeper)

Owner: Aaron Purewal

Nothing moves to Security, Legal, or Vendor Risk until this review is complete. Evaluates efficacy, technical AI specifications, business justification, and MNPI exposure.

✓ Approve If

  • Clear business case
  • No obvious redundancy
  • Specs acceptable or vendor willing to use Jefferies endpoints
  • POC with defined scope & data restrictions

⏸ Slow Down / Escalate If

  • Data hosted outside Jefferies AWS/Azure
  • Unclear subprocessors or restricted regions
  • Security hasn't reviewed AI functionality
  • MNPI exposure without controls

✗ Deny If

  • Blatant redundancy ("we have 50 products that do this")
  • Poor/outdated technology
  • Clear compliance violations
Hany's guidance: "Don't hold back. Balance velocity vs. governance. No-brainers, approve them. Tell requesters we're building a new process — they may need to do retroactive actions later."

Gatekeeper Checklist

  • Is Jefferies buying a tool/platform or licensing data? Different paths apply.
  • Research the tool online — confirm a basic understanding of how it's being used.
  • Has the tool been approved from the AI perspective? Check GEP/JEDI notes.
  • Business justification: Why can't existing tools be used?
  • LLM architecture: What models, where hosted, subprocessors?
  • Data handling: Retention policy, encryption, BYOK capability?
  • MNPI exposure: Does data leave Jefferies AWS/Azure?
  • Peer adoption: Used by Goldman, JPM, or Morgan Stanley?
  • Geographic restrictions: Users in mainland China? Flag for AI SME.
  • What countries will JEF users be located in? Check DPA requirements if outside US.

Regulatory & Audit Context

  • SEC and global regulators incoming (Dubai already painful)
  • Regulators: "Tell us how you're using every AI-enabled software"
  • Australian regulator audit inquiry in progress
  • Need clear accountability for AI outcomes
  • Annual recertification: unchanged usage? No additional data?
  • Priority: stop new request leakage, then document existing tools
Watch Out: POC contract friction, scope creep into Legal, questionnaire sign-off, and document everything for audit readiness.

Stage 2 — Security Review

Owner: Daniel / Alex

After AI Review approval, the vendor proceeds to Security for technical security assessment. Aaron provides security notes and conditions from Stage 1.

Security Review Scope

  • Penetration testing — vendor infrastructure and application layer
  • SOC2 Type II — current certification status and scope
  • Architecture review — data flows, subprocessors, hosting locations
  • FedRAMP authorization — if applicable for cloud services
  • Encryption — in-transit and at-rest, customer-managed keys (BYOK)
  • Access controls — admin-level model access, MFA, hardware key 2FA
  • Network controls — egress restrictions, endpoint allowlisting

Security Notes from AI Review (Passed Forward)

Standard conditions Aaron attaches when passing to Security:

Vendor SaaS / Hosted Tool

  • Dedicated tenant requirement
  • BYOK support
  • Zero-day retention on LLMs
  • Disclosure of all third-party models
  • Filter enable/disable capability

Jefferies-Hosted Tool

  • Must be hosted in Jefferies environment only
  • Vendor must redirect LLM API calls to Jefferies endpoints
  • No data egress outside firm infrastructure

Stage 3 — Legal Review

Owner: Amanda Kline + Adam (J-Fin)

After AI and Security approval, Legal reviews contract terms. Amanda's AI Cheatsheet defines the standard questions, contracting terms, and sample clauses for AI vendor agreements.

MNPI Default Policy: "No MNPI" for all AI contracts unless specific technical controls are confirmed. Complete prohibition on MNPI going outside the firm.

Legal Triage — "What Do I Do When I Receive an AI Contract?"

Jefferies Buying a Tool/Platform

  • Has it been approved from the AI perspective by Aaron Purewal?
  • Check GEP/JEDI for additional security terms
  • Is it vendor-hosted (SaaS) or Jefferies-hosted?
  • What countries will JEF users be located in?
  • If non-US users, check DPA requirements and server hosting location
  • If Anthropic agreement — flag for AI SME if users in unlisted countries

Jefferies Buying Data from a Vendor

  • Does the vendor platform have AI functionality available?
  • Does the vendor use AI to collect or process the data?
  • What countries will JEF users be located in?

Consultant / Managed Services (Not Staff Aug)

  • Is the vendor/consultant using AI tools to provide their services?
  • If yes — in their environment or Jefferies? Default: AI in JEF environments for JEF data.
  • Who operates the AI tools? Consultant only, Jefferies only, or both?

AI Contracting Terms — Sample Clauses

Standard terms Legal should include in AI vendor agreements (from Amanda's cheatsheet):

1. Dedicated Tenant

Vendor stands up a dedicated instance for Jefferies use only during the term.

2. Bring Your Own Key (BYOK)

All customer data encrypted using a Jefferies-managed key. Vendor cannot decrypt without Jefferies authorization.

3. Zero-Day Retention

LLM inputs/outputs not retained, stored, cached, or used for training. Transient processing only; deleted within 24 hours.

4. Third-Party Model Disclosure

Vendor must disclose all LLM providers, model names, versions, hosting regions. 30 days' notice before any model change.

5. Security Filters

Vendor provides admin controls for automated security filters on inputs/outputs. Filters cannot be disabled without Jefferies approval.

6. Hosting in JEF Environment

Platform hosted, deployed, and operated solely within Jefferies-controlled cloud (AWS/Azure/VPC/on-prem). No data egress.

7. API Redirect

All LLM API calls routed exclusively through Jefferies-designated endpoints ("Customer AI Endpoints"). No direct vendor-to-LLM calls.

8. MNPI Segregation

Strict logical and physical segregation of Jefferies MNPI. Tenant isolation, need-to-know access, background checks for vendor staff.

Additional Agreement Provisions

  • MNPI upload restrictions: JEF business teams not permitted to upload MNPI — this is an internal guardrail; exceptions need Compliance + IT Sec approval
  • MNPI segregation by vendor: Whether vendor can actually segregate MNPI depends on architecture — must be verified
  • Vendor audit/governance: Performance benchmarks, bias and risk assessment, audit rights
  • Termination rights (model changes): If vendor changes model providers, functionality, hosting locations
  • Termination rights (risk/compliance): If JEF determines vendor AI practices pose reputational risk or compromise security/regulatory compliance
  • Data retention variance: Check if LLM retention differs from the Information Security Addendum — vendors with third-party models may have different retention timelines
POC Friction: Vendors increasingly want full contract negotiations upfront, creating double-work for Legal. Duration: up to 6 months (flexible).

Stage 4 — Vendor Risk (Final Sign-Off)

Final Gate

After AI, Security, and Legal have all signed off, the vendor request moves to Vendor Risk for final enterprise risk assessment and approval.

Final Sign-Off Scope

  • Enterprise risk classification — tier determination based on data sensitivity and business impact
  • Compliance alignment — confirm adherence to Global Gen AI Policy and firm risk appetite
  • Contractual completeness — all AI-specific clauses, MNPI protections, and termination rights in place
  • Ongoing monitoring plan — annual recertification requirements defined
  • Audit trail — documentation complete for SEC and regulatory review

Post-Approval Requirements

  • Annual recertification: "Are you still using this the same way? No additional data?"
  • Change notifications: Vendor must notify 30 days before model or architecture changes
  • Ongoing monitoring: Usage tracking, incident reporting, compliance review
  • Hany cc'd on all approval/denial communications

Critical Policies (All Stages)

MNPI Policy

MNPI is NEVER allowed in anything public or web-based unless within Jefferies' AWS or Azure instance. Complete prohibition on MNPI going outside the firm.

Legal Default

"No MNPI" for all AI contracts unless specific technical controls are confirmed.

POC Standards

Up to 6 months duration. Vendors increasingly want full contracts upfront — creates double-work for Legal.
